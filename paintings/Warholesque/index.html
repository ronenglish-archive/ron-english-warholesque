Warholesque

Silkscreen ink, stencil, and synthetic polymer paint on canvas

A dedicated section for Ron English’s Warholesque paintings and related documented variants.

This body of work adapts Warhol-associated imagery, materials, and production methods through Ron English’s own visual language. Representative images and related documentation are gathered here to support comparison across versions and future research.

Works in the Warholesque theme may involve silkscreen, stencil application, or both, combined with extensive hand-painting in synthetic polymer paint. Each painting is completed in a distinct color combination, making every example unique.

Subjects associated with the series include Dandy Banana, Young Andy, MC Supersized, Marilyn variants with Mickey Mouse breasts, Elvis figures, Cowgirl Guernica, Bruce Lee, Figment, Super Sunflower, and Abraham Obama.

Process (typical workflow)

  1. Ron paints an original work, usually monotone and without a background.
  2. A high-resolution image is used to prepare a silkscreen and/or stencil.
  3. Canvases are cut and gessoed.
  4. The initial screen or stencil layer is applied as a guide.
  5. Ron paints each canvas in unique color combinations, using the applied image as a reference. Once completed, the guide layer is often no longer visible.
  6. Additional screen or stencil layers may be applied.
  7. The canvases are stretched.

The result is a unique Ron English painting that adopts Warholian production logic while asserting English’s own visual language and conceptual framework.
